Seven months ago, three employees of the Ecuadorian MISC newspaper El Comercio MISC were abducted in the Mataje River LOC region on the border between Ecuador LOC and Colombia LOC . After almost three weeks in captivity, reporter Javier Ortega PER , photographer Paul Rivas PER , and their driver, Efrain Segarra PER , were executed by the Oliver Sinisterra Front PER , a group of former FARC ORG guerillas and drug traffickers who operate in this lawless jungle. The murders punctuated a season of escalating violence, the likes of which Ecuador LOC hasn’t seen in decades.



The official version of events remains full of inconsistencies, and the victims’ families are still waiting for answers. Why was a government representative dispatched to the border to await the journalists’ release, only to return empty-handed? Why was the El Comercio MISC staff permitted in the area after El Guacho LOC , the guerilla leader, had openly threatened civilians? The police could hardly feign ignorance: They had a secret communications channel with the rebels, and the transcripts clearly show their intentions.



An international team of more than 20 reporters, photojournalists, and videographers worked for four months to investigate the abduction and murder of their Ecuadorian MISC colleagues, as well as to continue their reporting. Six organizations collaborated with OCCRP MISC on the project: Fundacion para la Libertad de la Prensa MISC ( FLIP ORG , Colombia LOC ), La Liga Contra El Silencio MISC ( Colombia LOC ), Periodistas sin ORG Cadenas LOC ( Ecuador LOC ), Fundamedios LOC ( Ecuador LOC ), Verdad Abierta PER ( Colombia LOC ), and Forbidden Stories MISC ( France LOC ).
